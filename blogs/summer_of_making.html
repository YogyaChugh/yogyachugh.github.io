450 Hours, 4 Ships, One Laptop

Summer 2025

I participated in the Hack Club Summer of Making, a global hardware and software building initiative sponsored by GitHub. During the 3-month period, I logged over 450+ tracked coding hours across 4+ shipped open-source projects.

The Grind

The goal wasn't just to write code, but to ship usable products. It meant long nights, dealing with complex bugs across different languages (Rust, C++, TypeScript), and forcing myself to learn new architectural patterns on the fly to meet deadlines.

The Results

By the end of the summer, I was ranked in the top tier on a highly competitive global leaderboard. As a merit-based prize for the hours logged and the quality of the projects shipped, I was awarded a $1,649 high-end laptop (RTX 5070, 32GB RAM, Ryzen AI 7).

More importantly, the experience permanently upgraded my ability to scope, build, and ship complete systems rapidly.
