450 Hours, 4 Ships, One Laptop

Summer 2025

I participated in the Hack Club Summer of Making, a global hardware and software building initiative sponsored by GitHub. During the 3-month period, I logged over 450+ tracked coding hours across 4+ shipped open-source projects.

My goal wasn't just to write code, but to build complete systems and products. The rigorous building schedule forced me to dive deep into new frameworks, manage my time aggressively, and debug complex infrastructure issues on the fly.

At the end of the summer, I ranked in the top tier on the competitive global leaderboard, earning a $1,649 laptop (RTX 5070, 32 GB RAM, Ryzen AI 7) as a merit-based prize.

More importantly, the experience permanently upgraded my ability to scope, build, and ship complete systems rapidly.
